Reflection Questions & Answers

1. What does the Detection Zone simulate in a “smart environment”?
It simulates real-world physical sensors, such as motion detectors, proximity sensors, or cameras, that trigger automated actions when a person enters a specific physical area.

2. How does the NPC decide when to move?
The NPC continuously calculates the mathematical distance between its position and the Player's position. It decides to move only when this calculated distance drops below a predefined value (chaseDistance).

3. What changes made the environment feel more responsive?
Providing immediate multisensory feedback—such as changing the ambient light color and playing an alert sound the moment the player entered a zone—combined with the NPC dynamically reacting to the player's proximity, made the environment feel alive and responsive.

4. If you had real AI, what would you improve in this scene?
With real AI, I would add intelligent pathfinding (like Unity NavMesh) so the NPC can navigate around obstacles instead of walking in a straight line. I would also add dynamic decision-making, where the NPC could choose to hide, interact, or predict the player's movements using machine learning.
